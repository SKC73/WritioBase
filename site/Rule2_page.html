Show, Don't Tell Tutorials
Part I: Evocation

Tutorials are numbered according to strand flow and coloured according to tutorial type (white for brief lectures, light purple for writing exercises, blue-grey for quizzes). Learners are free to skip around, taste, try, mix and match. You design your course based your needs and temperament.

1. Warm-up

Use a picture prompt to write a 5-minute story.

2. Introduction: Evocation

Writers will consider what "evocation" is and how it relates to writing fiction.

3. Definition

We will come to a working definition for what "evocation" is and how it can be applied to our writing.

Quiz

Test your understanding!

4. Exercise

Writers revise their warm-up written exercise to emphasize "evocation."

5. Evoking emotions

Evocation involves the emotions. But what is an emotion where writing is concerned?

6. Example Emotion

We see how evoking an emotion can propel our writing.

7. Wrap-up: Writing the non-zero state

We connect the dots between emotions, evocation, and good writing.

8. Final Writing Exercise

Now it's your chance to show what you learned with a final writing exercise.

What we are about

There are classes about writing by writers for writers. There are classes by teachers for students who are writers. This is a website about writing by teachers who are writers and writers who are teachers. Which means, it's a website for anyone who wants to learn to write without feeling intimidated by the teacher.
